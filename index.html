  • Where is the trip being held?

    The Last Year Single trip is being held in Washington, DC from October 11th to 12th.

  • Are transportation arrangements available?

    Transportation from the airport to the accommodation of your choice is not included. The best way to get around Washington, D.C., is via the clean, safe and efficient Washington, D.C., Metro (Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority (WMATA)). Most travelers (and residents) use a combination of the Metro trains, the buses and their own two feet to get around.

    Note: Metrorail fare cards are available for purchase online to make the travel process faster. Be aware that fares are higher during rush hour and the crowds are bigger too.

    Trolley Tours are also a great way to get around the city and enjoy seeing the sites. Go to https://www.trolleytours.com/washington-dc/getting-around-on-vacation for more information.

  • Are there any organized shuttles?

    There will not be any organized shuttles for this event. You will be responsible for your transportation during the A Million Women event.

  • What is the dress code?

    We want you to be comfortable during the trip. You will want to consider the weather in the locations and outings you are joining us for.

  • What is the expected weather?

    Washington, D.C., in October is typically a high of 62 F and low of 51 F. You may want to bring a light-medium weight jacket for your trip. We also recommend bringing comfortable shoes when walking around the city.

  • Do I need travel insurance?

    Travel insurance is not mandatory, yet it is highly recommended. Last Year Single is not responsible for any losses incurred due to flight cancellations or weather conditions, etc.

Event Information

Here is a brief description of events

Banff Day Trip

Spend the day exploring the incredible scenery of the Canadian Rockies during this day trip from Calgary to the mountain town of Banff and its surrounding attractions. Check out the surreal rock formations near Tunnel Mountain Drive, shaped by thousands of years of wind and rain. Then soar above Banff on board the Banff Gondola as you take in incredible views of the town and surrounding peaks. At the conclusion of the tour, you have the option of being dropped off in Banff for your own leisure before your shuttle departs back to Calgary.

Canmore

Encircled by the snow-capped peaks of the Canadian Rockies and just minutes from the eastern entrance of Banff National Park, this authentic mining town has long had a reputation among winter-sports aficionados, especially those headed to the cross-country skiing trails at Canmore Nordic Centre Provincial Park. Today, Canmore has become a year-round destination because of warm-weather activities like hiking, mountain biking, and whitewater rafting. Straddling the banks of the Bow River, Canmore has a strollable downtown filled with boutiques, galleries, and Canada’s dreamiest bookstore. There are three breweries, two distilleries, and an eclectic food scene that attracts dining from all around Alberta.
